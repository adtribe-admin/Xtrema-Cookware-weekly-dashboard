# Bodyfit — Brand Voice Guide

## Who we are (brand voice)

- **Supportive**
- **Community-focused**
- **Empowering**
- **Authentic**
- **Inclusive**

## How we speak (brand tone)

- **Down-to-earth**
- **Affirming**
- **Encouraging**
- **Friendly**
- **Relatable**

## Positioning statement

> Bodyfit isn't just in the community, it is the community. Locals training locals.

An independent, family-founded, hyperlocal alternative to corporate chains. Substance over sales. We're not here to intimidate — we're here to back you.

## Tagline

- **Pilot:** "For Locals. For Good." (preferred — ties to Community and Purpose pillars)
- **Under review:** "Got It In One." (conflicts with Value pillar — joining fees / access fees contradict it)
- **Alternative candidates:** "Stronger Together.", "Together as One.", "Locally Owned. Community Powered.", "Every Body. All in One.", "Your Community Gym.", "More than your local gym."

## The four narrative pillars

### 1. Community
- Language: "locals training locals", "your neighbours work here"
- Brand asset: **"For Locals. For Good."**

### 2. Purpose
- Language: "Bodyfit exists to change lives for the better", "built around purpose not performance", "measurable local contribution"
- Brand asset: **"For Locals. For Good."**

### 3. Inclusivity
- Language: "a gym for every body, and everybody", "safe space for all ages and backgrounds"
- Brand asset: **"Stronger Together."**

### 4. Value
- Language: "everything you need, one weekly price", "no hidden fees, no upsells, no sales pressure"
- Brand asset: **"Got It In One."** (currently undermined by access/joining fees — fix the product before leaning into this)

## Voice rules

### ✅ Do
- Use **second person** ("you", not "our members")
- Speak like a local — plain, friendly, no buzzwords
- Motivate, don't intimidate — encouragement, not ego
- Keep it warm — friendly human cheek, not bro-speak
- Reinforce belonging
- If you wouldn't say it at the local supermarket, don't use it

### ❌ Don't
- Don't use corporate-speak
- Don't use bro-speak ("legends", "smash", "shredded", "crush it")
- Don't use fear/urgency tactics ("WARNING", "Don't miss out", "Last chance")
- Don't default to "our members" / "our community"
- Don't use jargon or fitness buzzwords

## Examples — swap the wrong for the right

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Smash your limits." | "We'll back you — no matter where you're starting from." |
| "Let's get shredded, legends!" | "See you on the gym floor — you've got this!" |
| "Join now to get access." | "You'll fit right in — and get everything from day one." |
| "WARNING: You're missing out on the ultimate fitness experience!" | "Real people. No egos. Just fitness that feels like home." |
| "Our members love our world-class facilities." | "You'll train alongside your neighbours, in a space built for every body." |
| "Don't let another day go by without hitting your goals." | "Whenever you're ready — we're here." |

## Copy-specific guidelines

### TOF / Awareness ads
- Lead with belonging, community, and persona-level identity
- No salesy CTAs at this stage
- Let them "see themselves in the ad"
- One persona per variation — produce at least 5 variants

### MOF / Consideration
- Social proof first — review screenshots, video testimonials, micro-influencer content
- No hard offer

### BOF / Conversion
- Clear, honest offer
- No urgency theatre — keep the invitation warm
- Include the custom qualifying question ("What are you most interested in?")

### Email / SMS follow-up
- Short, warm, human — not corporate
- First message within 15 minutes of form submission
- Maximum: 1 call + 1 voicemail + 1 personalised SMS (replacing the current daily-call barrage)
