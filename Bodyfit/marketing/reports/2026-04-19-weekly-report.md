**Google Ads**

👉 **Key Metrics**
**Leads:** 25
**Cost per Lead:** $26.48
**CTR:** 12.86%
**Total Spend:** $662
**Form submits only:** 25 ($26.48 cost per form submit)

👉 **Performance Overview**
Google delivered **stable volume at a softer CPL**, with lead count flat week-over-week but cost per lead rising as spend increased.
• Leads flat at 25 → 25
• CPL rose from $22.10 → $26.48
• CTR improved from 12.15% → 12.86%
• Spend increased from $552 → $662 (+20%)
The higher CTR paired with flat conversions signals **increased click volume is not translating into more form submits** — a funnel / landing page friction point worth investigating.

**Key Insights**
• **One campaign (23600468596) is driving 88% of form submits** — 22 of 25 — and is the clear efficiency engine. The other three campaigns combined delivered only 3 submits.
• **Form-start to form-submit ratio is a concern** — 49 form starts generated only 25 submits (~51%), meaning roughly half of engaged users drop off mid-form.
• CTR climbing above 12.8% shows **keyword intent and ad relevance are strong** — the leak is post-click, not pre-click.
• Flat conversion volume with rising spend suggests **diminishing return on the current bidding approach** — worth testing tCPA caps or a conversion-value-based strategy.
